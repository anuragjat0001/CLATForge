Mock Analysis Protocol

The mock itself is 25% of the value. The analysis is 75%.

Step 1: Simulation

Attempt the mock strictly timed (2 hours). No pausing. Simulate exam conditions.

Step 2: Perception

Before seeing answers, write down: perceived difficulty, uncertain questions, estimated score.

Step 3: Categorization

Categorize every wrong answer: Concept Gap, Application Error, Careless Mistake, Time Pressure, or Trap Selection.

Mistake Elimination System

Mistake 1: Studying content without attempting questions

Start attempting questions on Day 1 of each topic. You learn the subject through questions, not theory alone.

Mistake 2: Ignoring Current Affairs until the last 3 months

CA is 26–28% of CLAT. Daily 45-minute CA habit from Day 1 is non-negotiable.

Mistake 3: Taking mocks but not analyzing them

Analysis is where learning occurs. A student who takes 50 mocks with deep analysis will outperform a student who takes 100 mocks with no analysis.

Mistake 4: Attempting all questions regardless of accuracy

Selective, high-accuracy attempts win. Develop your personal "attempt-or-skip" threshold for each subject.
