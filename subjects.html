Subject-Wise Master Strategy

Deep dive into each section of the CLAT exam

4.1 ENGLISH LANGUAGE (RC Focus)

CLAT English is entirely passage-based. 22–24 questions from 4–5 passages. Every minute must be spent on passage work.

Passage Mastery Framework

Step 1: Active Reading Protocol

Mentally tag: Main Argument | Evidence | Counterargument | Author's Stance | Key Terms. Write a 2-line summary of each paragraph.

Step 2: Question Sequencing Strategy
  1. Vocabulary-in-context
  2. Direct factual retrieval
  3. Main idea/theme
  4. Inference questions
  5. Para-jumble/sentence completion

Speed Benchmarks

4.2 CURRENT AFFAIRS & GK

Covers 18 months of current events. Tests events in passage context — requires understanding, not just memorization.

Source Architecture

  • Daily: The Hindu (Front page, National, International, Economy, Edit page).
  • Weekly: The Week or Frontline magazine.
  • Monthly: Vision IAS or Vajiram & Ravi Monthly Current Affairs.

Static GK Focus

  • Constitution: Articles 12–35, 51A, 72, 74, 79, 100–111, 123, 148–151, 214–237, 280, 312, 315–323, 324–329, 352–360.
  • Polity: Laxmikanth (Chapters 1–10, 18, 19, 22, 26, 28, 29, 30, 31, 32, 33).
  • History: Modern Indian history (1857–1947).

4.3 LEGAL REASONING

Tests your ability to apply a stated legal principle to a factual scenario. Background familiarity improves speed and accuracy.

High-Frequency Topics

  • Law of Torts (Negligence, Vicarious Liability, Nuisance)
  • Contract Law (Formation, Breach, Damages)
  • Criminal Law (Mens rea, Actus reus, Murder vs. Culpable Homicide)
  • Constitutional Law (Fundamental Rights, Writs)

The 4-Step Application Method

  1. Read the Principle precisely.
  2. Read the Fact situation completely.
  3. Map the facts to the principle.
  4. Eliminate answer options that ignore either.

4.5 QUANTITATIVE TECHNIQUES

Strategic Approach: Minimum Effort, Maximum Marks. 10–13 questions total.

Non-negotiable topics:

  • Ratios and Proportions
  • Percentages
  • Averages
  • Profit and Loss
  • Data Interpretation (Graphs, Tables, Pie Charts)

Data Interpretation: 2 DI sets per day from Phase II onwards. Practice speed arithmetic.
